OBJECT DETECTION ON FUSED DATA

Besides my studies, I work at BMW in Unterschleissheim where I am involved in various projects in the field of autonomous driving. Most of the development is done in Python, but C++ is also used. I often work with different sensor data, such as lidar or image data, and evaluate, develop or improve neural networks that provide valuable information such as object positions from this data.

REAL TIME ROAD ESTIMATION

In summer 2019, I completed a five-month internship at BMW in Shanghai, China. In particular, i focused on the development of a machine learning-based approach for "online map validation". This involves estimating the course of the road based on sensor data and deciding whether the stored map of the environment is valid or invalid, or which part of the map is out of date. In the course of this, my colleagues and I published a paper at the IEEE Intelligent Vehicles Symposium - IV2020 entitled "Real-time long-range road estimation in unknown environments".

OPTICAL FLOW FOR LANE CHANGES

I wrote my bachelor thesis on "Camera-Based Vehicle Detection and Optical Flow Estimation for Real-Time Lane-Change Decision using Deep Learning", at BMW in Munich. A new neural network architecture is presented that not only improves the accuracy of object detection, but also enables the estimation of the optical flow. The optical flow describes the relative motion of each pixel within an image. This makes it possible to predict whether a safe lane change will still be possible in the near future.
